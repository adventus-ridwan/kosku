# Kosku — Public Experience v1.0

---

# Purpose

The public experience exists to help prospective tenants become familiar with a boarding house before they decide to visit or stay.

Unlike traditional boarding house platforms that primarily display listings, Kosku guides visitors through an experience that gradually builds understanding, familiarity, and trust.

The objective is simple:

> Help people imagine themselves living there.

---

# Core Principle

People do not choose a room first.

They choose a place where they can imagine living.

Everything shown in the public experience should answer one simple question:

> "Would I feel comfortable living here?"

---

# Visitor Journey

Every visitor should naturally move through the following journey.

Landing

↓

Introduction

↓

Interactive Boarding House Experience

↓

Story

↓

Neighborhood

↓

Moments

↓

Room Types

↓

Contact Owner

Every section increases familiarity and confidence.

---

# Landing Page

Purpose:

Create curiosity.

The landing page should be visually attractive but intentionally simple.

Visitors should immediately understand:

- what this boarding house is
- why it is different
- why they should continue exploring

Sections:

- Hero Image
- Boarding House Name
- Tagline
- Three Highlights
- Short Introduction
- CTA:
  - Jelajahi Kos
  - Masuk Owner

Landing should never feel crowded.

Its only goal is encouraging visitors to continue.

---

# Boarding House Experience

This is the heart of Kosku.

The Boarding House Experience is not a list of information.

It is an exploration.

The interactive map becomes the primary interface.

Visitors should feel like they are walking around the property.

---

# Interactive Map

The interactive map should resemble the real property as closely as possible.

Visitors should immediately recognize:

- entrance
- parking
- stairs
- kitchen
- prayer room
- shared facilities
- gardens
- room positions

Every important object should be interactive.

---

# Information Card

When an object is selected, the information panel updates.

Example:

Gate

- photo
- description
- opening hours
- notes

Parking

- photo
- capacity
- description

Kitchen

- photo
- facilities
- usage information

Room

- room type
- availability
- preview
- price

The map should never display large popups.

The information card changes naturally while the visitor explores.

---

# Story

The Story section explains:

- why the boarding house exists
- its philosophy
- what makes it unique
- who it is suitable for

Stories should feel personal rather than promotional.

---

# Neighborhood

The Neighborhood section helps visitors understand daily life around the property.

Examples:

- restaurants
- laundry
- convenience stores
- mosque
- campus
- public transportation

Owner recommendations should be highlighted.

---

# Moments

Moments show life inside the boarding house.

Examples:

- studying together
- community cleaning
- breaking fast
- relaxing in common areas

Moments create emotional confidence.

---

# Room Types

Room Types explain the available living options.

Rather than presenting every room individually, visitors first understand the available room categories.

Individual rooms inherit their presentation from their Room Type.

---

# Contact

The final section encourages visitors to take action.

Display:

- WhatsApp
- phone number
- address
- visiting hours

The primary CTA should always be:

> Hubungi Owner

---

# Mobile First

Most visitors will use smartphones.

The experience should feel natural on mobile.

Interactive elements should be touch-friendly.

Scrolling should feel smooth.

The experience should never resemble an admin interface.

---

# Design Principles

The public experience should feel:

- welcoming
- modern
- calm
- visual
- trustworthy

Avoid:

- technical language
- management terminology
- excessive tables
- unnecessary forms

Visitors are exploring a place.

Not using management software.

---

# Relationship with Workspace

Workspace is the authoring environment.

The public experience is the presentation layer.

Workspace creates.

Public presents.

Workspace manages.

Public inspires.

No public information should exist independently.

Everything displayed publicly originates from Workspace.

---

# Future Evolution

Future versions may include:

- Guided Tour
- Interactive Layers
- Reviews
- Alumni Stories
- Availability Calendar
- Booking
- AI Guide

These features should enhance the existing experience rather than replace it.

---

# Success Criteria

The public experience succeeds when visitors say:

"I already understand this boarding house."

"I can imagine living here."

"I want to contact the owner."

Not because they saw more information.

But because they felt more familiar with the place.
