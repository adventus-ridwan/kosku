# Kosku — Product Vision v2.0

---

# Vision

Kosku is an interactive boarding house platform that helps owners manage their business while helping prospective tenants feel familiar with a boarding house before they decide to live there.

Kosku is not built only to manage rooms.

It is built to tell the story of a place.

---

# Why Kosku Exists

Most boarding house platforms help people find available rooms.

Kosku aims to help people understand and experience a boarding house before they ever visit it.

Choosing a boarding house is not simply choosing a room.

People are choosing where they will spend months or even years of their lives.

They want to understand more than:

* price,
* room size,
* facilities.

They also want to know:

* where the entrance is,
* how the building is organized,
* what the surrounding environment feels like,
* how the common spaces are arranged,
* whether they can imagine themselves living there.

Kosku exists to reduce uncertainty before the first visit.

The better someone understands a place, the easier it becomes to trust it.

---

Build the most intuitive visual boarding house management platform while creating the most trustworthy boarding house exploration experience for prospective tenants.

# Core Product Principles

## 1. The Boarding House Experience is the Product

The interactive map is the heart of the Boarding House Experience.

It helps prospective tenants understand the spatial layout of a boarding house, but it is only one part of a larger experience that also includes stories, neighborhoods, moments, room types, and contact with the owner.

The goal is not simply to display a map.

The goal is helping prospective tenants feel familiar with a boarding house before they arrive.

The interactive map is not just a navigation component.

It is the primary interface of Kosku.

Owners manage the boarding house from the map.

Prospective tenants understand the boarding house through the same map.

Every important workflow should naturally begin from the map whenever possible.

---

## 2. Maps Represent Reality

The digital map should resemble the physical boarding house.

Owners are encouraged to create layouts that closely match reality.

Prospective tenants should recognize:

* entrance
* parking area
* stairs
* hallways
* kitchen
* shared bathrooms
* prayer room
* laundry area
* gardens
* room positions

The closer the digital map resembles reality, the stronger the tenant's confidence becomes before visiting.

---

## 3. Workspace is the Source of Truth

Every piece of information is managed inside Workspace.

Workspace owns:

* boarding house profile
* room types
* rooms
* contracts
* tenants
* galleries
* stories
* facilities
* amenities

Public pages never own independent data.

They only display information managed by Workspace.

---

## 4. Public is a Projection

The public experience is not another application.

It is a projection of Workspace.

Workspace edits.

Public presents.

Workspace manages.

Public inspires.

Workspace stores.

Public explains.

---

## 5. Tell Stories, Not Just Data

Data explains.

Stories convince.

Instead of only saying:

> 12 m² room.

Kosku encourages owners to explain:

> Bright room with a garden-facing window, ideal for studying during the day.

Stories build emotional connections.

---

## 6. Build Trust Before Arrival

The goal of Kosku is not simply helping users contact an owner.

The goal is making prospective tenants say:

> "I already feel familiar with this boarding house."

Trust before arrival is the ultimate user experience.

---

## 7. Simple Over Complete

Version 1 focuses on solving real problems for small and medium boarding houses.

Every feature must provide meaningful value.

Complexity should only be introduced when it solves a real problem.

---

## 8. Privacy by Design

Private information must remain private.

Public users never see:

* tenant identities
* contracts
* financial information
* management controls
* unpublished rooms

Privacy is a fundamental design principle, not an optional feature.

---

## 9. Portfolio Quality

Kosku is built with production-quality engineering practices.

Every module should demonstrate:

* maintainability
* scalability
* readability
* thoughtful architecture

The project should serve as a professional software engineering portfolio.

---

## 10. Marketing Before Management

Operational tools help owners stay.

Public experience helps owners grow.

Workspace exists to help owners build a compelling public boarding house experience.

Every workspace feature should either improve operational efficiency or improve how prospective tenants understand the boarding house.

If a feature does neither, it should be reconsidered.

# Core Problems Solved

## Problem 1 — No spatial context

Traditional systems rely on tables.

Kosku keeps spatial understanding at the center through an interactive map.

---

## Problem 2 — Operational fragmentation

Tenant information, contracts, and room management are often scattered across spreadsheets and messaging apps.

Kosku centralizes them.

---

## Problem 3 — Poor discoverability

Many boarding house platforms expose only basic information.

Kosku allows owners to present:

* stories
* environment
* facilities
* room types
* maps

instead of only prices.

---

## Problem 4 — Lack of trust

Prospective tenants often hesitate because they cannot visualize the property.

Kosku reduces uncertainty through realistic visual representation.

---

## Problem 5 — Manual repetitive management

Managing dozens or hundreds of rooms individually is inefficient.

Room Types allow shared information to be managed once and reused everywhere.

# Anti Goals

Kosku is not intended to become:

* A generic CRUD application.
* A marketplace with thousands of anonymous listings.
* A spreadsheet replacement with maps attached.
* A feature-heavy platform without clear product direction.
